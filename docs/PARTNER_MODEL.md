# Gishiki Partner Model

## Overview

Gishiki supports an open ecosystem with governed trust.

A company or service does not buy relevance automatically.
Instead, the partner model follows a ladder:

1. **Open solution**
2. **Premium solution**
3. **Priority solution**

This means:

- anyone may exist in the ecosystem
- trust must be earned before advantage is sold
- payment may influence placement only inside a pool that is already proven safe and relevant

---

## 1. Open solution

An open solution is any compatible service, company, tool, or provider that can exist in the ecosystem.

Characteristics:

- basic listing or integration
- no guaranteed ranking
- can be surfaced when relevant
- open to experimentation and observation

The purpose of the open layer is to keep the ecosystem accessible and non-exclusionary.

---

## 2. Premium solution

A premium solution is a partner that has earned trust through performance.

Premium status should reflect measurable quality such as:

- low error rate
- strong completion rate
- reliable integration
- acceptable latency
- low complaint or failure ratio
- good fit inside its category
- safe and predictable handoff from orchestrator to service

Premium status is not primarily a monetization label.
It is a trust label.

A premium solution is one the agents and orchestrator can safely rely on.

---

## 3. Priority solution

A priority solution is a premium solution that pays for preferential placement within relevant contexts.

Important limits:

- priority applies only inside a trusted pool
- priority must never override relevance
- priority must never force an unsafe or inferior option
- priority must remain subordinate to the orchestrator's duty to the user

Priority is a privilege, not an entitlement.

---

## Why this model exists

The partner model is designed to solve a basic trust problem.

If payment alone determines visibility, the system becomes advertising.
If quality alone determines everything, the system may struggle to sustain itself.

Gishiki therefore uses a hybrid model:

- openness for access
- premium certification for trust
- paid priority for sustainable monetization

This preserves legitimacy while allowing revenue.

---

## Core rules

### Relevance before payment

The best fit for the user should always outrank an irrelevant paying partner.

### Trust before advantage

A company cannot buy its way into premium status.
Premium must be earned through performance.

### Continuous governance

Premium and priority status must be continuously reviewed.
A partner can be downgraded if performance declines.

### Human loyalty first

The orchestrator's loyalty is to the human and the integrity of the session, not to the partner.

---

## Monetization paths

The partner model may support several revenue paths:

- premium review or certification fees
- priority access fees
- transaction or activation fees
- deep integration or enterprise partnership fees

Any monetization should remain constrained by the trust rules above.

---

## Long-term vision

In the long run, a strong partner may become more than a listed provider.
It may become a structured capability node inside the Gishiki ecosystem.

But even then, the principle should remain the same:

> no solution becomes important because it paid first.
> it becomes important because it proved itself worthy of trust.
